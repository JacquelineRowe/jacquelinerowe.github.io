I am a first-year PhD Student in the [UKRI Centre for Doctoral Training in Designing Responsible Natural Language Processing](https://www.responsiblenlp.org/) at the University of Edinburgh. I am interested in hate speech detection for low-resource languages and multilingual LLM safety and alignment. I am supervised by Alexandra Birch and Shannon Vallor. 

Before my PhD, I worked on online content governance in the Legal and Policy Team at [Global Partners Digital](https://www.gp-digital.org/) and the [International Law Programme](https://www.chathamhouse.org/about-us/our-departments/international-law-programme) at Chatham House. I have conducted research on low-resource machine translation, NLP for creoles, the rights of linguistic minorities, and language policy and practice in Guinea-Bissau.

I have an MSc in Computer Science with Speech and Language Processing from the University of Sheffield (2024, Distinction), a Masters in Human Rights from Birkbeck University (2021, Distinction), and a BA in Linguistics from the University of Cambridge (2019, Double First).
